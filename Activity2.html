MOALBOAL

Moalboal, officially the Municipality of Moalboal ( Cebuano: Lungsod sa Moalboal; Tagalog: Bayan ng Moalboal ), is a fourth class municipality in the province of Cebu, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 36,930. Moalboal is one of the eight municipalities comprising the 7th Congressional District Cebu Province.

Moalboal Cebu is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life. It is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling, with its abundant coral reefs and diverse marine ecosystem.



GEOGRAPHY

Moalboal is a small town on the south-west side of Cebu Island. The town sits at the base of a peninsula which runs north from there and has an assortment of tourist facilities — resorts, dive schools, bars and restaurants — along much of its west coast.


DEMOGRAPHICS

The municipality has a land area of 124.86 square kilometers or 48.21 square miles which constitutes 2.53% of Cebu's total area. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 36,930. This represented 1.11% of the total population of Cebu province, or 0.46% of the overall population of the Central Visayas region. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 296 inhabitants per square kilometer or 766 inhabitants per square mile.


ECONOMY

Moalboal is a peninsula and therefore it is almost entirely surrounded by water. The majority of the people who live in the flat lands engage in fishing as their main mode of livelihood. Those who live in the mountain regions like Agbalanga, Bala, Batadbatad, Buguil and Busay live through farming.
